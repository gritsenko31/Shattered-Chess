How the Board Breaks
Fracture: Capturing a piece cracks the square, turning it yellow.

Severe Fracture: A second capture on the same square turns it red.

The Abyss: Moving off a red square makes it collapse into an Abyss.

Sight Blocked: Abysses block rooks, bishops, and queens.

Leapers: Knights jump over Abysses.

Fatal Landing: Nothing can land in an Abyss.

En passant, castling, and promotion work as in standard chess. Inspired by the mechanics described at shattered‑chess.netlify.app.

About the Game
Shattered Chess transforms the familiar 64‑square board into a fragile battlefield. Every capture doesn’t just change the material balance—it reshapes the terrain. As squares collapse into Abysses, you must constantly re‑evaluate lines, king safety, and long‑range domination.

Why It’s Different
Tactical board changes: Each capture slowly “destroys” the board instead of just moving pieces.

Spatial awareness: Abysses cut off diagonals and files, forcing you to think about open and closed paths.

Knight dominance: Knights ignore Abysses, making them especially powerful in late‑game wreckage.

Classic core: Standard chess rules apply, so any player can jump in quickly.
